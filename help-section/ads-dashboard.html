The Dashboard is the central place for sellers to see the performance of the campaigns. The ads dashboard consists of several subsections including.





The Nav Bar



The Nav bar primarily allows sellers to add funds, view transaction history of the funds that were added or log out.



Transaction History

The Transaction History displays the list of credit and debit transactions towards advertising, made by the seller.

Any funds that a seller adds towards ads or any funds used towards displaying ads are shown as credits and debits here.

Debits are done on a daily basis, so if all of products promoted spent about Rs 5000 today, you will see a Rs 5000 debit on the Transaction history page.

Credits are done by adding funds.



The Top bar



The top part of the dashboard consists of

Add Products: To add more products and start advertising them. Search box: search for particular products by SUPC or name,

Date range selector: To select date range for which the dashboard reports needs to be shown. Default is the last one month.

Show hidden / Deleted products: In case you chose to delete products from the dashboard, you re-enable them temporarily by clicking on “show hidden products” or by re-adding product.

Download reports: SUPC report which shows each SUPC and its impact due to ads.



The Data Table



This is the primary information dashboard which shows the Products and its SUPCs, The name and other important ad metrics.

Ad metrics that are shown include:

1. Impressions: The number of times this product was shown to the end user as an ad.
2. Clicks: The number of clicks this particular SUPC incurred.
3. CPC or Cost per click: The price snapdeal is charging you to promote this product. This is automatically decided when you add products.


Editing the Cost Per Click

1. However, you can edit the cost per click by clicking on the actual value. This will turn it into a text box that allows you to edit the value.



2. Average CPC: This is the average Cost Per Click that you have paid for this product, through the duration of the ad campaign.

3. Conversions: This shows the total number of Sales that you have incurred directly from the impact of this advertising campaign.

4. Sales (Rs) shows the total GMV / Sales incurred due to the promotion of that product.


Status of Products and Ads

Product and ads statuses are shown in data table as a “status” column.

Here are the statuses that you will see and the respective meaning

STATUS MEANING
No funds left This will be a common status for all products when you run out of money to run ads.
All products will stop getting promoted during this time.
What to do next? Add funds to be able to start advertising.
Out of stock This means the particular row / SUPC is out of stock.
What do do next? Update product stock availability and your promotions will automatically start.
Rating is less than 4.5 This means your seller rating is below 4.5 and all ads will automatically get paused, even if you have funds.
What to do next?
Improve your rating and your ads will resume automatically once rating has increased to 4.5
Not Live It means your products are not sold on snapdeal anymore.
What next?
You can delete to hide these products from the dashboard.
Paused It means your product is paused.
The status can be changed by clicking on Paused and changing the status to “Running”.

Deleted This is a deleted product. You can add it back into the dashboard and start promoting it by selecting “Add Products”
Running This is an actively promoted product.
You can pause or delete it from the dashboard by clicking on Running.


Deleting Products from Dashboard

Actively promoted products can be deleted from the dashboard by clicking on “Running” status. This will show an option to delete the products.



You may want to delete products from the dashboard if you don’t want to track the product or promote it anymore.

The deleted products are removed from the dashboard.

You can still however see them by selecting “Show deleted products” from the top bar.

This information on past campaign performances is retained due to audit purposes, hence the products are not removed entirely from the ads system upon delete.
